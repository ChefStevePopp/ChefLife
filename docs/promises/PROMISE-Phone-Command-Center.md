## The Problem

Restaurant managers check their phones 100+ times per shift. Most apps make that painful:

- **Cramped navigation** ‚Äî 6 tiny buttons fighting for space
- **Desktop interfaces on mobile** ‚Äî Shrink everything down, hope for the best
- **Feature lists, not workflows** ‚Äî Menu ‚Üí Submenu ‚Üí Submenu ‚Üí Action
- **Context blindness** ‚Äî App doesn't know you're on shift, who's working, what needs attention

You pull out your phone, tap around, hunt for what you need, accomplish one task, put it away. Repeat 100 times. Each interaction takes 30 seconds when it should take 5.

## The ChefLife Way: The Launcher Paradigm

Your phone isn't a shrunk desktop. It's a **command center**.

### Three Pillars: People, Place, Profit

Swipe between three contextual pages:

| Page | Focus | You Ask |
|------|-------|---------|
| **People** | Team | Who's on? Who's late? Who needs coaching? |
| **Place** | Operations | Temps logged? Tasks done? Receiving ready? |
| **Profit** | Money flow | Invoice entered? Counts done? Revenue tracking? |

Each page has:
- **Contextual hero** ‚Äî What matters RIGHT NOW
- **Widget accordions** ‚Äî Glanceable stats, expandable details
- **Icon clusters** ‚Äî 4-6 quick actions with glassmorphism styling

### Contextual Intelligence

The shell knows:
- **Who you are** ‚Äî Your role determines what you see
- **When you're working** ‚Äî Shift status in header
- **What needs attention** ‚Äî Alert ticker scrolls urgent items

A line cook sees: Temps, Tasks, My Schedule.
A manager sees: Full team, All operations, Profit tools + Admin.

## The Hierarchy: People, Place, Profit

This isn't random. It's **restaurant philosophy**:

> "If you don't have the people, you don't have a place for guests. No guests, no profit. Take care of 1 and 2 ‚Äî 3 follows if you're not daft."

‚Äî Steve Popp

The app structure mirrors how successful restaurants actually think:

1. **People first** ‚Äî Are we staffed? Is the team healthy?
2. **Place second** ‚Äî Is the kitchen running? Are standards met?
3. **Profit third** ‚Äî Money is the outcome, not the input

Software that starts with profit metrics trains operators to think backwards.

## The UX Principles

### No More Menus

Menus are for restaurants, not apps. A menu says "here are 20 things you might want." A command center says "here's what matters RIGHT NOW."

### Thumb-Friendly Everything

- Big tap targets (48px minimum)
- Primary actions within thumb reach
- Swipe navigation, not buried buttons

### Context Over Configuration

The app should know:
- Your shift ends at 9:30pm (show it)
- GFS delivers on Tuesdays (remind you)
- Emily is new (surface coaching opportunities)

Less setup, more intelligence.

### Role-Based, Not Disabled-State

If you can't use it, you don't see it. No greyed-out buttons taunting line cooks with manager features. Clean, focused, appropriate.
