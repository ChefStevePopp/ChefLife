## What Are Promises?

Promises are the **value commitments** we make to our users. They capture:

- The real-world problem we're solving
- Why existing solutions fail
- How ChefLife does it differently
- The tangible benefit to the operator

Every feature should connect to a Promise. If it doesn't serve a Promise, why are we building it?

---

## Promise Structure

Each Promise document follows this format:

```markdown
# PROMISE: [Title]

## The Problem
What pain are operators experiencing? Be specific. Use real scenarios.

## Why Existing Solutions Fail
How do competitors or manual methods fall short?

## The ChefLife Way
How do we solve this? Connect to specific features.

## The Benefit
What does the operator gain? Time? Money? Peace of mind?

## The Proof
How do we demonstrate this value? Metrics, testimonials, demos.

## Connected Features
Which ChefLife modules/features deliver this promise?
```

## Writing New Promises

When you discover a value proposition during development:

1. Create a new `PROMISE-[Name].md` file
2. Follow the structure above
3. Connect to the Core Philosophy
4. Add to this index
5. Link to relevant ROADMAP items

Promises emerge from:
- Customer pain points
- Competitive analysis
- "Aha!" moments during development
- Steve's 35 years of industry knowledge

## The Memphis Fire Philosophy

> "People over profit, smiles over savings, compassion over commerce"

Translated to software:
- Don't make them do repetitive work
- Learn from them, not just store their data
- Respect that they have lives outside the restaurant
- Build a tool that gives time back, not takes it
